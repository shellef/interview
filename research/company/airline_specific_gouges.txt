=== DELTA AIR LINES ===

INTERVIEW DAY FORMAT:
- Starts at 6:45 AM sharp — be early, dress professional
- MMPI psychological assessment (~570 questions) — answer truthfully, don't overthink
- Panel interview (45 min to 1 hour)
- Panel: typically 2 Delta pilots + 1 HR representative
- Conversational format, tailored to individual background
- Lunch break between groups

QUESTION TYPES:
- TMAAT (Tell Me About A Time) — behavioral
- WWYD (What Would You Do) — scenario-based
- Resume-specific questions
- "Tell us about yourself / why Delta"
- NOTE: No technical questions reported in recent gouges

PROCESS NOTES:
- Group sizes ~18 applicants — high offer rate ("all but one got a CJO")
- "Be yourself, be truthful" — interviewers reward genuine responses
- Watch for nonverbal cues from panelists to gauge when to stop
- Professional logbook printing recommended
- Book hotel early — rooms near Atlanta HQ fill fast

REQUIREMENTS:
- Minimum 1,500 flight hours (ATP minimum)
- Bachelor's degree or equivalent
- Competitive candidates typically 3,000-5,000+ hours

CULTURE:
- Delta focuses heavily on CRM, professionalism, and long-term commitment
- Strong emphasis on TMAAT format — prepare 8-10 solid stories

=== SOUTHWEST AIRLINES ===

INTERVIEW FORMAT:
- In-person in Dallas, TX
- Three sections: HR Interview, Logbook Review, Scenario-Based Questions
- Business attire required
- Bring: tabbed logbook, resume, flight hour totals, checkride records

LOGBOOK REVIEW (casual conversation with captain):
- Total flight time, cross-country, PIC, night, instrument, multi-engine
- Know your 90-day and 12-month totals, monthly averages
- Any checkride failures — be honest, explain what you learned

HR SECTION (captain + HR rep):
- "Why Southwest?" — know the culture deeply
- "What will you bring to Southwest?"
- Behavioral questions using START method (Situation, Task, Action, Result, Takeaway)
- Conflict resolution, service excellence, leadership failures, proud moments

SOUTHWEST'S THREE CORE VALUES:
1. Warrior Spirit — work hard, desire to excel, be courageous
2. Servant's Heart — follow the Golden Rule, treat others with respect
3. Fun-LUVing Attitude — have fun, don't take yourself too seriously, celebrate successes

SCENARIO-BASED QUESTIONS (sample):
1. "You're approaching Dallas Love Field with thunderstorms building. ATC offers a vector to final, but your FO expresses concern about windshear reports..." (78% of interviews)
   → Show: crew communication, dispatch coordination, go-around decision making, safety over schedule

2. "During preflight, you discover a discrepancy that's not in the logbook but the previous crew mentioned it verbally..." (65%)
   → Show: MEL knowledge, maintenance coordination, documentation importance, not flying with undocumented discrepancies

3. "Ground crew is rushing to make a 25-minute turn, but you notice they haven't completed the weight and balance paperwork..."
   → Show: authority to hold departure, safety over on-time performance

CULTURE-FIT QUESTIONS (sample):
1. "Southwest is known for making flying fun. Describe a time you turned a stressful situation into a positive experience..." (92%)
2. "Tell us about a time you went above and beyond to help someone, not because it was your job..." (84% of successful candidates)
3. "A flight attendant approaches you before boarding, clearly overwhelmed by personal issues..."

KEY METRICS:
- Panel: 2 pilots + 1 HR rep
- Scoring: 40% technical, 35% cultural alignment, 25% leadership
- Culture-fit questions: 40% of interview (vs. ~15% at legacy carriers)
- 73% of 2024 hires came from regional airlines
- Year 1 pay: ~$69/hour; Year 8: ~$168/hour
- Process duration: 4-6 weeks total

TIPS:
- "Be relaxed, friendly, and professional"
- Interviewers WANT you to succeed — they're not trying to trip you up
- Southwest differs substantially from legacy carriers — emphasize personality and customer service more than technical mastery

=== UNITED AIRLINES ===

SOURCE: Justin Ritter (United Airlines Chief of Hiring) insights from YouTube (uY-UPlpy6Pw)

Key themes from United's hiring perspective:
- Looking for people who are team players and safety-focused
- CRM is extremely important — demonstrate assertiveness and communication
- They want pilots who will be leaders, not just followers
- Preparation and professionalism matter — show you've done your homework
- United values diversity and international experience

Process typically includes:
- Initial application and screening
- Video interview (HireVue or similar)
- In-person assessment day: cognitive tests, sim eval, panel interview
- TMAAT questions heavily weighted
- Technical knowledge tested (varies by candidate background)

=== AMERICAN AIRLINES ===

Process (general):
- Online application with PRIA, FOIA requests required
- Cognitive/personality assessment
- In-person interview day in Dallas (HQ)
- Panel interview: mix of TMAAT and WWYD
- Simulator evaluation
- Technical interview component (stronger than Delta's)

Known emphasis:
- CRM philosophy — how you handle disagreements with captain
- Safety culture — will you speak up?
- Professionalism and appearance

=== ALASKA AIRLINES ===

Process:
- Strong emphasis on the "Alaska Way" — their culture document
- Panel interview with TMAAT format
- Technical questions — relatively more technical than Southwest/Delta
- Simulator evaluation

Culture focus:
- Genuine care for customers and coworkers
- Safety above all
- Humility and continuous learning

=== REGIONAL AIRLINES (General) ===

Common regionals feeding to majors: SkyWest, Envoy, Mesa, Horizon, GoJet, CommutAir, PSA

Format typically simpler:
- Shorter interview day (half day)
- TMAAT questions + basic technical
- Simulator or aircraft knowledge check
- Often faster hiring decisions

Key differences from majors:
- Less emphasis on company-specific culture prep
- More emphasis on basic CRM and technical knowledge
- Good place to build interview experience before major airline attempt

=== INTERVIEW PROCESS OVERVIEW (ALL AIRLINES) ===

Typical 7-stage process:
1. Online Application — resume, logbook, certificates, background check authorization
2. Aptitude Tests — cognitive ability, spatial reasoning, personality
3. Technical Interview — 20-40 min of aircraft systems, met, regs
4. HR/Behavioral Interview — STAR method competency evaluation
5. Simulator Assessment — 30-60 min flight evaluation (basic handling + CRM)
6. Group Exercise — problem-solving and teamwork (mainly cadet programs)
7. Final Interview — cultural fit, commitment, Q&A

HOUR REQUIREMENTS:
- US legacy carriers: 1,500 hours ATP minimum; competitive at 3,000-5,000+
- US regionals: 1,500 hours ATP minimum
- European flag carriers: 1,500+ hours typical
- Middle Eastern (Emirates, Qatar): 3,000-4,000+ hours
- Cadet programs: Zero hours required

SIMULATOR ASSESSMENT — what evaluators look for:
- Basic handling skills and instrument scanning
- Procedural adherence and checklists
- CRM and communication with crew
- Decision-making (go-around vs continue judgment)
- Ability to learn unfamiliar equipment quickly
- Ability to accept coaching and make corrections

ELIMINATING MISTAKES (common reasons for rejection):
- Lack of airline-specific research/knowledge
- Negative comments about previous employers
- Poor presentation or grooming
- Overconfidence or arrogance
- Weak CRM demonstration
- Inconsistency between application and interview answers
- Dishonesty about checkride failures
